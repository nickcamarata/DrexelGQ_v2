RETRO-ACTIVE IS ALL THE RAGE

By Charlotte Kenny-Yap

March 14th, 2018

Sportswear meets serious menswear for top trends in 2017 and 2018 men’s fashion. Synonymous with this trend is “athleisure.” It's been the hype for some years now, but this new take is outdated sportswear brands reinvented with logos, colors, cuts, shapes, and silhouettes. We’re seeing brands like Adidas, Fila, Reebok, Champion, and Tommy Hilfiger come full circle revisiting their 80’s and 90’s vibes. Think matching tracksuits, vintage sneakers, baseball jackets, vintage jerseys, nylon jumpers, and drawstring trousers in various luxury style fabrics. So what are some staples of this trend and where did they come from? Here’s a little background of its history:

FOOTWEAR

     Today’s sporty, amped-up sneakers get much of their influence from classic sneaker brands like Adidas and Nike as well as from footwear brands like Fila and Reebok that offered vintage trainers with classic silhouettes for years. These styles have now traded up and are coming back in full swing, being revamped with added edge in the color, shape, and textiles.

The 80s

     The 80’s had the “shell suit,” the matching two piece zipper tracksuit, and it was all anyone wanted in the 80’s when it came to looking “dope”. Hip-hop was on the rise and these pieces went hand in hand with rap groups and stars of the day. The hip-hop group, Run-DMC, iconically wore this look and young people took this trend by storm mix-matching colors, patterns, and styles. This style is coming back little by little.

The 90s

     90’s rave culture was truly the rave and inspired baggier and brighter oversized fits in almost all areas of fashion. Wearing XXL only was the rule. Inspired by techno raves and acid houses, this party culture took fashion by storm with brands like Champion and Fila pioneering the trend in sweatshirts, jackets, and pants.

RETRO SPORTSWEAR T-SHIRTS

     Vintage football jerseys, hockey jerseys and basically all jerseys are making a real comeback. Real vintage limited edition jerseys from the 70’s, 80’s, and 90’s are being worn as fashion today and are being auctioned at very high prices. These nitty-gritty one-of-a-kind jersey styles are being appropriated in fashion through jersey style t-shirts with sports inspired colors, patterns, emblems, and numbers.

TRACKSUITS

     The tracksuit, is noticeably one of the most iconic sportswear staples. Not only is it engrained in popular culture, but also fashion. Originally the tracksuit was intended from athletic use with its nylon shiny material and loose fitting. Worn by music and style icons like Eminem and Drake, the tracksuit has made its name fashion. Even high fashion designers like Balenciaga have even created collections with oversized silhouettes and quirky looks inspired by the original piece.

    So why is this trend so prevalent today? Sportswear very much has found its way into the fashion world for decades now representing core values in fashion like on-the-go comfort, authenticity, high quality, and timeless style with an edge. Athletic culture is only growing and has now engrained itself in fashion with people wanting to look and feel the part while also representing a sporty chic ambiance. High fashion brands are reinventing the style as well transforming the vintage pieces into extremely coveted clothing.

BOTTOM LINE

     Sportswear is on the rise and is here to stay.
